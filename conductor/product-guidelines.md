# Product Guidelines: Industrious Flight School

## Voice & Tone

### Overall Tone
**Warm & Encouraging** — Flight School should feel like a supportive guide walking alongside new team members. The tone is welcoming, celebrates progress, and creates psychological safety during what can be an overwhelming time for new hires.

### Addressing Users
Use **direct second person** throughout the product:
- ✅ "You've completed 3 modules"
- ✅ "Your next training starts tomorrow"
- ✅ "You're making great progress"
- ❌ "The user has completed..."
- ❌ "We've updated your profile..."

## Visual Identity

### Brand Colors
Adhere to the Industrious brand palette:

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **Deep Teal** | `#013E3F` | Primary background, headers |
| **Dark Teal** | `#012d2e` | Sidebar, secondary backgrounds |
| **Warm Cream** | `#F3EEE7` | Text on dark backgrounds, cards |
| **Golden Yellow** | `#FDD344` | Accents, active states, CTAs, highlights |

### Design Principles
- Maintain sufficient contrast for accessibility
- Use golden yellow sparingly for emphasis and interactive elements
- Cream text on teal backgrounds for primary content areas

## Content Writing

### Action-Oriented Language
Always guide users toward their next step. Focus on what they *can do*, not just what exists.

| Instead of... | Write... |
|---------------|----------|
| "Module available" | "Start your next module" |
| "Training scheduled" | "Join your training session" |
| "Task incomplete" | "Complete this task to continue" |
| "Progress: 50%" | "You're halfway there — keep going!" |

### Keep It Scannable
- Use short sentences and clear headings
- Break complex information into bullet points
- Lead with the most important information

## Terminology

### Required Terms

| Term | Usage | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| **New Bees** | Friendly term for new hires | Use in UI labels and casual contexts |
| **New Hire** | Formal alternative | Use in reports, formal communications |
| **Flight School** | The onboarding program name | Always capitalize both words |

### Avoid
- "Onboardee" or "trainee" — use "New Bee" instead
- Overly corporate jargon
- Passive voice when action is needed
